# APA 7th Edition — Webpage & Website Reference Formats

**Authority:** https://apastyle.apa.org/style-grammar-guidelines/references/examples/webpage-website-references  
APA Publication Manual, 7th Edition, Section 10.16, pp. 350–352

---

## Core Format

```
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of page in sentence case. Website Name. URL
```

Elements in order:
1. Author(s) — Last, Initials. | Organization Name.
2. Date — (Year, Month Day) | (Year, Month) | (Year) | (n.d.)
3. Title — *italicized*, sentence case
4. Website Name — not italicized, omit if same as author
5. URL — no period after URL

---

## All Cases

### Standard: Individual Author
```
Bernstein, M. (2002, January 8). 10 tips on writing the living web. A List Apart: For People Who Make Websites. https://alistapart.com/article/writeliving
```

### Standard: Group/Organization Author
```
Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. (2020, March 28). Preventing COVID-19 spread in communities. https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/community/index.html
```

### Author = Website Name (omit site name)
```
American Psychological Association. (2019, October 30). Expanding the circle of caring. https://www.apa.org/monitor/2019/10/circle-caring
```

### No Date (n.d.)
```
Author, A. A. (n.d.). Title of page. Website Name. URL

Minnesota Department of Health. (n.d.). Flu activity and surveillance. https://www.health.state.mn.us/diseases/flu/flu-surveillance.html
```

### No Author — Title First
```
Title of page. (Year). Website Name. URL

Herd immunity and COVID-19 (coronavirus): What you need to know. (2020, November 24). Mayo Clinic. https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/coronavirus/in-depth/herd-immunity-and-coronavirus/art-20486808
```

### Retrieval Date (use only for pages that change over time and are not archived)
```
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of page. Website Name. Retrieved Month Day, Year, from URL

U.S. Census Bureau. (n.d.). U.S. and world population clock. U.S. Department of Commerce. Retrieved July 3, 2019, from https://www.census.gov/popclock/
```

### Webpage with Individual Author on News Website
```
Schwartz, J. (2019, August 13). Ousted chief says Fox News broke with him over a refusal to air partisan segments. The New York Times. https://www.nytimes.com/2019/08/13/business/media/fox-news-editor-ousted.html
```

### Social Media — Twitter/X Post
```
Author, A. A. [@handle]. (Year, Month Day). First 20 words of the post [Tweet]. Twitter. URL

Britt, M. A. [@PsychFiles]. (2019, August 9). I just finished watching season 3 of #DarkNetflix. Excellent show. Just as good as seasons 1 and 2 [Tweet]. Twitter. https://twitter.com/PsychFiles/status/1159839551802920960
```

### Social Media — Instagram Post
```
Author, A. A. [@handle]. (Year, Month Day). Caption/title [Photograph/Video]. Instagram. URL

University of Southern California [@uscedu]. (2019, February 20). Great to have @CurbedLA take a look around the @lacoliseum today with USC VP of Auxiliary Services Dan Stimmler [Photograph]. Instagram. https://www.instagram.com/p/BuH0pbNAC2R/
```

### Social Media — Instagram Highlight (always n.d.)
```
Author [@handle]. (n.d.). Title of highlight [Highlight]. Instagram. Retrieved Month Day, Year, from URL

The Conscious Kid [@theconsciouskid]. (n.d.). Rethinking Dr. Seuss [Highlight]. Instagram. Retrieved March 16, 2020, from http://bit.ly/2wXO1NN
```

### YouTube Video
```
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of video [Video]. YouTube. URL

BBC Earth. (2016, January 8). Why do cats meow? [Video]. YouTube. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qeUM1WDoOGY
```

## Field-by-Field Rules

### Author
- Individual: Last, F. M. (initials only after first author if more than one)
- Up to 20 authors listed; 21+ → first 19, ellipsis, last author
- Group author: spell out fully on first reference; abbreviate in subsequent in-text

### Date
| Situation | Format |
|---|---|
| Full date known | (2023, April 14) |
| Month and year only | (2023, April) |
| Year only | (2023) |
| No date | (n.d.) |
| In press | (in press) |

### Title
- Sentence case (capitalize only first word, proper nouns, and first word after colon)
- Italicized for standalone works (webpages, reports, whole sites)
- NOT italicized for articles within a larger source

### Website Name
- Written as it appears on the site
- Omit if the same as the author name

### URL
- Copy the full URL
- No period after the URL
- Do not add "Retrieved from" unless a retrieval date is needed
- For DOIs: format as https://doi.org/10.XXXX/XXXXX

---

## Validation Checklist (APA7 Web)

For `valid` status, a webpage citation must have:
- [ ] Author (individual, group, or title-first substitute)
- [ ] Date (year, fuller date, or n.d.)
- [ ] Italicized title
- [ ] Website name (unless author = site)
- [ ] URL or DOI
